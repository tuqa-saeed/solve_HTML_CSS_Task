Amblyopia(Lazy Eye)

what is lazy eye?

Lazy eye, also known as amblyopia, is a condition where one eye has poorer vision than the other, despite the eye being structurally normal. It occurs when the brain favors one eye over the other, leading to reduced vision in the weaker eye. Causes: Strabismus: Misalignment of the eyes (e.g., one eye may turn in, out, up, or down). Refractive errors: Significant differences in the vision of each eye, such as nearsightedness or farsightedness. Deprivation: Conditions that block the visual input to one eye, like a cataract or eyelid drooping.
